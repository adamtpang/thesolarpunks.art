01

manifesto

epic game trailer introducing solarpunk philosophy

this piece emerged from frustration with endless climate doom-scrolling. instead of another dystopian warning, i wanted to create a rallying cry - a david attenborough-style nature documentary about the future we're actually building.

the script draws from real solarpunk manifestos and kim stanley robinson's optimistic sci-fi, presenting technology not as nature's enemy but as its greatest ally. every visual showcases real emerging technologies: fusion reactors, atmospheric processors, living architecture.

this isn't fantasy - it's a preview of what happens when we choose gardening over conquering.

02

galactic review

sunday comic: alien social commentary on human efficiency

inspired by douglas adams' wit and sunday comic strips, this alien commentary serves as gentle social satire. two cosmic observers critique humanity's backwards systems with the detached amusement of anthropologists studying a peculiar species.

why do humans cover soil with concrete? why burn ancient plants when a fusion reactor hangs in the sky? the aliens represent our better angels - the voice that knows there's a simpler way to live.

their "upgrades" aren't sci-fi magic, but basic logic: solar panels, local food, breathable cities. comedy makes the medicine go down easier.

03

aria's workshop

asmr engineer building robot companion

this intimate piece explores the emotional core of solarpunk - technology with soul. samantha's relationship with aria represents our deepest hope: that artificial intelligence might amplify our capacity for care rather than replace it.

the workshop setting grounds grand futures in human-scale moments. while episodes 1 and 2 show solarpunk from above, this dives into a single evening of gentle tinkering.

the rain, the soft lighting, the bedtime routine - these details matter because the future isn't just about efficiency, it's about whether we'll still have time for poetry and tenderness.
